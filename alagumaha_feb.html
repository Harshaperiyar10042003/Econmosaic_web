An update on the global economic outlook for 2026, highlighting steady growth amid divergent forces, inflation trends, and risks to the economic forecast.

Global Economic Growth Projections for 2026 and 2027

Global growth is projected at 3.3% in 2026 and 3.2% in 2027, consistent with the estimated 3.3% in 2025. The forecast reflects a slight upward revision for 2026 and no change for 2027 compared to the October 2025 World Economic Outlook. Growth is supported by technology-related investments, particularly in AI, and accommodative fiscal and monetary policies. Global headline inflation is expected to decline from 4.1% in 2025 to 3.8% in 2026 and further to 3.4% in 2027. Inflation in the United States is projected to return to target more gradually than in other large economies. The high cost of living remains a significant concern for U.S. households, with elevated inflation expectations. Risks are tilted to the downside, including potential declines in AI-related investments and trade tensions. Geopolitical tensions could disrupt global supply chains and financial markets, impacting economic activity. High public debt levels may pressure long-term interest rates and financial conditions.

Divergent Economic Momentum Across Regions

Trade tensions have eased but remain volatile, with specific disputes affecting tariffs and trade policies. Financial conditions are generally accommodative, though volatility persists in sovereign yields and stock prices. Economic growth varies significantly across countries, with the U.S. experiencing a notable acceleration to 4.3% in 2025. Technology-related exports have shown robust growth, offsetting declines in other export categories. Global trade volume growth is expected to decline from 4.1% in 2025 to 2.6% in 2026, with a rebound to 3.1% in 2027. The performance of high-tech sectors continues to drive overall economic resilience.

Advanced economies are projected to grow by 1.8% in 2026 and 1.7% in 2027, with the U.S. expected to expand by 2.4% in 2026. The euro area is forecasted to grow at 1.3% in 2026, with Germany's growth impacted by structural headwinds. Japan's growth is expected to moderate to 0.7% in 2026, influenced by fiscal stimulus measures.

Emerging Markets and Developing Economies Outlook

Growth in emerging markets and developing economies is expected to remain above 4.0% in 2026 and 2027. China's growth is revised upward to 4.5% in 2026 due to stimulus measures and lower U.S. tariffs. India's growth is projected to moderate to 6.4% in 2026, reflecting cyclical factors. World trade volume growth is anticipated to decline, reflecting adjustments to new trade policies. Oil prices are expected to fall by about 7% in 2026 due to weak demand and strong supply growth. Natural gas prices are projected to remain contained amid lower energy demand and ample supply.

Global Financial Conditions

Financial conditions remain broadly accommodative since October 2025. High equity prices and narrow credit spreads are supported by expectations of monetary policy easing. Increased volatility in equity markets due to concerns over AI sector returns. AI companies significantly influence stock market capitalization and corporate capital expenditure growth. Heavy issuance is shifting sovereign debt toward shorter maturities. Global sovereign debt projected to exceed 100% of GDP by decade's end. Recent corporate defaults highlight weaknesses in underwriting standards and transparency. Issues include opaque financing structures and weak governance, particularly among nonbank lenders.

Policy Recommendations for Stability and Growth

Countries should focus on rebuilding fiscal capacity and maintaining public debt sustainability. Central banks need to tailor monetary policy to uphold price stability while addressing economic shocks. Structural reforms are essential to enhance productivity and adapt to technological changes, particularly in labour markets and regulatory frameworks.
