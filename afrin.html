Role of Kudumbashree in Strengthening Local Self-Governance and Participatory Planning

Local Self Government Department, Government of Kerala

India’s flourishing women-centered Kudumbashree Mission was initiated by the Kerala Government in 1998. In the direction of poverty reduction through social participation under the State Poverty Eradication Mission. The word “Kudumbashree” in Malayalam means “family overall well-being,” which indicates the focus on women’s empowerment aimed at social and profitable development. The Kudumbashree Mission is organized with a three-league structure. Neighborhood Groups (NHGs) are organized usually at the grassroots, Area Development Societies (ADSs) at an area or ward level, and Community Development Societies (CDSs) at the local self-government level. This democratic governance is in every village and local municipality in Kerala, with women’s active involvement in strategic planning, problem-solving, and community empowerment initiatives. From 2011 and beyond, Kudumbashree has been a part of the State Rural Livelihoods Mission (SRLM) under the National Rural Livelihoods Mission (NRLM). Incorporating 40 lakh women for membership, which is considered as one of the largest collectives of women's organizations anywhere in the world. It has been instrumental in facilitating 2.23 lakh neighborhood groups with live bank linkage loans amounting to almost ₹9000 crore and has facilitated over one lakh micro-enterprises across major sectors such as catering, construction, apparel farming, and digital services. These initiatives have increased women's financial autonomy and leadership and have significantly contributed to Kerala's progress towards achieving gender empowerment and local decentralized governance.

Kudumbashree is also initiating new ideas to link women entrepreneurs to open digital commerce platforms like the Open Network for Digital Commerce (ONDC) to enhance market access and visibility for rural products. It also supports a National Resource Organization (NRO), which works with sixteen states to replicate the Kerala model on their own terms. Research studies have highlighted Kudumbashree’s efforts to improve women's decision-making, health awareness, and social mobility (Down To Earth, 2023; Journal of Family Medicine & Primary Care, 2019). More than just poverty alleviation, Kudumbashree is also a movement where women are collectively reimagining community development, entrepreneurship, and governance from the ground up.
