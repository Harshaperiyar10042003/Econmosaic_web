"India's Economic Direction in 2025–2026: Survey Highlights and Analysis"

The Economic Survey 2025–2026 was tabled in Parliament on January 29, 2026 by Finance Minister. It was prepared by the Department of Economic Affairs under the Ministry of Finance, guided by the Chief Economic Advisor.

The Survey observes that global policymaking is increasingly driven by security and political priorities rather than efficiency or multilateral cooperation. Geopolitical tensions have intensified global economic conditions, with concerns such as the weaponization of energy and finance, export controls on technology, skepticism toward free trade, and growing competition over critical minerals shaping the international landscape.

Amid these global challenges, India has emerged as the largest and fastest-growing major economy for the fourth consecutive year. This performance reflects the resilience of the Indian economy and timely government interventions that have sustained growth momentum. The Survey projects an expected GDP growth rate of 7.1 percent in the financial year 2027.

India has also avoided the fiscal stress experienced by several advanced economies, thereby maintaining greater stability in global borrowing markets compared to countries such as the United States, Germany, and the United Kingdom.

In sectoral performance, the agricultural sector recorded a 1.5 percent decline in Gross Value Added (GVA) compared to the previous year. However, the other two major sectors, along with overall GDP, have registered increases, though not at an exceptionally high rate. The Survey highlights improvements in capital accumulation, labour input, and total factor productivity as key contributors to GDP growth. Both the primary deficit and fiscal deficit have declined compared to the previous year and are expected to reduce further by FY27.

GST Reform 2.0 has supported higher consumption of manufacturing goods. GST revenue collections have increased, indicating a reduction in revenue leakages. The Reserve Bank of India has pointed to lower interest rates and enhanced liquidity in the financial system. Public awareness and accessibility to life and non-life insurance have improved. The expansion of the Atal Pension Yojana has increased participation from workers in the unorganized sector. The adoption of artificial intelligence within the Reserve Bank of India underscores the growing importance of AI in India's financial system. Progress in microcredit has also been observed through the PM-SVANidhi scheme, which empowers street vendors through access to credit.

External Sector

India continues to experience a trade deficit with other countries; however, strong performance in the services sector has helped offset this imbalance. Several new foreign trade agreements are underway, and India has attracted increased foreign investment, particularly in the digital sector.

Inflation

Consumer Price Index (CPI) inflation has declined and remains among the lowest compared to major global economies. Favourable monsoon conditions have been cited as a key reason for the lower CPI. Higher core inflation has been attributed to elevated prices of precious metals such as gold and silver etc. Prices in the manufacturing sector appear relatively weaker when compared to the agricultural and services sectors.

Industrial Sector

Medium and high industries account for approximately 66 percent of India's industrial sector. The GVA of core industries has increased. Domestic production of automobiles, coal, and mobile phones has risen, while imports, particularly of mobile phones, have declined. India's innovation ecosystem has strengthened through the growth of startups, increased patent applications, and improvements in the Global Innovation Index ranking.

Infrastructure and Investment – Strengthening Connectivity, Capacity, and Competitiveness

There has been an increase in project appraisals by the Public–Private Partnership (PPP) appraisal committee. The total national highway network has expanded by 60 percent, with more high-speed corridors becoming operational, and the railway network continuing to grow annually. Port infrastructure has expanded, although domestic container production remains low. To address this, the budget announced exemptions from customs and basic customs duties for container production. In the power sector, electricity generation has increased. Overall capacity and transformation capability of electricity have improved. Revenues in the power sector and household electricity supply have increased. Through the BharatNet initiative, optical fiber connectivity has expanded to gram panchayats, enhancing high-speed broadband access. Under the Jal Jeevan Mission, 81 percent of rural households now have tap water facilities.

Environment and Climate Change

The Survey emphasizes the strategic importance of rare earth minerals for renewable energy production and storage. It highlights the need to strengthen mining activities and reduce import dependence on critical minerals to support renewable energy sources such as solar and wind power. India is projected to require approximately 411 GW of energy storage capacity by 2031–32. The budget has allocated ₹20,000 crore in pilot funding to establish five indigenous Small Modular Reactors (SMRs) to reduce carbon emissions. However, specific data on CO₂ emissions was not detailed in the Survey.

Education and Health

The Gross Enrollment Ratio has increased, and multilingual education is being promoted through AI-driven digital content in multiple languages. Learner-centric education reforms are being strengthened through the introduction of APAAR ID (Automated Permanent Academic Account Registry). Additionally, 100 percent Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) is permitted in higher education.

Health, Employment, and Rural Development
Health

Infant Mortality Rate (IMR) has declined, and life expectancy has improved. Protein and calorie intake have increased in both rural and urban areas. At the same time, obesity is rising, partly due to sedentary lifestyles influenced by AI-based technologies. Mental health concerns have increased, especially due to digital addiction. Health infrastructure has been strengthened through increased funding and initiatives. The Budget 2026–27 proposes the establishment of mental health centers in all district health centers.

Employment and Skill Development

Labour force participation and unemployment rates are expected to improve with the implementation of new labour codes. Skill development initiatives include expanding ITIs, upgrading Industrial Training Institutes, promoting apprenticeship schemes, and implementing PMKVY 4.0 to connect employers with job seekers. Innovation funding has also increased through various initiatives highlighted in the Survey.

Rural Development and Social Progress

Progress has been made in reducing extreme poverty. Infrastructure improvements aimed at enhancing rural and social development have been emphasized in the Survey 2025–26. The Survey also underscores the importance of the AI ecosystem for India’s labour market.

Human Capital, Data, and Urbanization

The Survey stresses the need to strengthen human capital in artificial intelligence by adopting advanced learning methods to build a robust AI ecosystem in the coming years.

Data as a Strategic Resource

With nearly one billion internet users, India generates vast amounts of human-generated data. Ensuring greater domestic value retention of this data is essential. Government initiatives are encouraged to promote growth and development in this sector while minimizing foreign influence over citizens’ data.

Urbanization

Urban India requires substantial expansion of public transportation systems. Transit-oriented development, along with value capture mechanisms around transit stations, is recommended. Progress in waste management has been noted in Indian cities. The Survey advocates for strengthening civic discipline through structured rules, incentives, and system-based awareness rather than occasional messaging. It also suggests implementing distributional urban policies and behavioural reforms to support sustainable urban development.
