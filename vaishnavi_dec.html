Transit-Oriented Development for Urban Sustainability

India's first transit-oriented development (TOD) project, the 48-story "Towering Heights" in East Delhi, signifies a change from car-centric planning to integrated, sustainable urban life.

Did you know?
According to the TOD urban planning concept, High-density, mixed-use construction is encouraged in the vicinity of public transport hubs, such as metro or train stations. In contrast to car-centric urban sprawl, it seeks to make public transport the centre of city development.

Aim
The main objectives are to encourage walkable, pedestrian-friendly communities; lower pollution levels and commuting expenses; shorten travel times and distances; and lessen reliance on personal vehicles.

Significance of TOD for Urban development
TOD reduces commutes, decreases the use of cars, and improves air quality by developing walkable, transit-connected neighbourhoods. In order to preserve open spaces and maximise urban land usage, TOD promotes vertical, compact expansion around transit stations (500–800 m radius).
Although the metro system requires a lot of resources, TOD guarantees strong patronage by developing densely around stations. Additionally, it makes value capture financing possible, which uses increasing land values to finance building and upkeep.
TOD increases productivity while reducing carbon emissions, travel times, and car use. Transit-based growth reduced emissions by 35% and increased economic value by 41% in Stockholm (1993–2010).
By encouraging greater densities and job clustering, TOD improves city competitiveness; a 5–10% increase in economic productivity can result from doubling work density.

World Bank’s 3V for TOD
Node Value – A station's significance in the public transportation system is based on its central location, passenger volume, and intermodal links.
Place Value – Based on walkability, urban block size, land use diversity, service accessibility, and neighbouring facilities, it represents the station area's quality and appeal.
Market potential Value – Based on factors such as employment density, transit accessibility, housing density, development land, zoning scope, and market activity, it shows the market potential of station areas.

Implementation Steps for Sustainable Urban development
Incorporate TOD with consistent land use, density, and parking standards into municipal plans, zoning laws, and regulations. In large cities, establish a Unified Metropolitan Transport Authority (UMTA) to coordinate housing, transportation, and urban planning. The UMTA should have institutional procedures for coordinated execution and interagency collaboration.
Provide rental or hostel lodgings close to transportation hubs for employees and students, and reserve housing for Economically Weaker Sections (EWS) and Low-Income Groups (LIG) in TOD zones for social inclusion.
Plan TOD zones using GIS mapping and data analytics, and establish performance measures such as decreased travel, increased ridership, walkability, and social mix for ongoing observation.

In order to tackle sprawl, traffic, and pollution, transit-oriented development is an essential paradigm shift for sustainable urban India. In order to create truly liveable cities, the National TOD Policy 2017 offers a blueprint, but its success depends on resolving institutional, financial, and inclusivity issues through integrated governance, creative finance, and people-centric design.
