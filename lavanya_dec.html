INDIA - NEW ZEALAND FREE TRADE AGREEMENT

India and New Zealand announced a Free Trade Agreement (FTA) to strengthen its economy. They started to negotiate this agreement in March 2025 and concluded on 22nd December 2025.

A deal both sides are calling historic, not just for what it covers, but for how quickly it comes together. In just 9 months, the 2 Countries sealed an agreement aimed at deepening economic ties, opening up markets and reshaping how businesses, professionals and students move between these 2 Nations.

The gain from the agreement, for India, aligns with its broader trade strategy of forging balanced and mutually beneficial partnerships, particularly in the Indo-Pacific region. For New Zealand, India represents a large and rapidly growing market with increasing demand for goods, services, and investment opportunities. India has offered market access in 70.03% of the tariff lines covering 95% of Bilateral Trade Value while keeping 29.97 % tariff lines in exclusion.

The highlight of the agreement is liberalization of Goods and Services between 2 countries. India receives zero custom duty on 100% exports boosting jobs MSMEs and Manufacturing. This ensures immediate competitiveness for Indian products across labour-intensive manufacturing and in pharmaceutical, chemical, engineering, and agri-processed sectors, reducing landed costs and expanding export opportunities.

India fully protects dairy, sugar, oils, species, metals and rubber from import pressure. The products that are kept in exclusion are mainly- Dairy (milk, cream, whey, yoghurt, cheese etc.), animal products (other than sheep meat), vegetable products (onions, chana, peas, corn, almonds etc.), sugar, artificial honey, Animal, vegetable or microbial fats and oils, Arms and Ammunition, Gems and Jewellery, Copper and Articles (Cathodes, Cartridges, Rods, Bars, Coils etc.), Aluminium and articles thereof (Ingots, billets, wire bars) among others. 30.00% of tariff lines will have immediate duty elimination, covering wood, wool, sheep meat, leather-raw hides etc.

Advancing Agricultural Productivity and Farmer Incomes, while Protecting India’s Sensitive Sectors, New Zealand is delivering innovative Agriculture Productivity Action Plans for India’s kiwifruit, apples, and honey to improve productivity, quality, and sectoral capabilities.

New Zealand has committed to facilitating USD 20 billion in investment into India over 15 years, thereby supporting manufacturing, infrastructure, innovation and job creation and also New Zealand has introduced its first ever annexes on health, traditional medicine, student mobility and Post-study work.

India secures access in 118 service sectors including Information Technology (IT), Education, Tourism and Construction. This agreement provides 5000 temporary work Visas, working holiday and post study options. Three years of work visas and renewable under certain conditions, eligible fields include IT, Engineering, healthcare and education. There is demand for unique Indian professions like AYUSH practitioners (Ayruveda, Yoga, Unani, Siddha, Homeopathy), Yoga instructors, Indian Chefs and Music teachers.

These visas required job offers and skilled roles facilitating services trade in 118 sectors open to Indian providers. Now if we compare this with Australia they gave only 1000 annual work visas and the United Kingdom (UK) did not offer anything close to this. So far India’s side this was a huge win. More jobs, more exposure, more global mobility of Indian talent.

India is no longer chasing big flashy trade deals. India is choosing selective caution in strategic agreements. Which protects farmers, pushes services, exports talent and attracts long-term investments.

This Free Trade Agreement (FTA) designed to reshape the sectors, Textiles and clothing, a cornerstone of India’s export economy, stand to benefit from zero duty market access with immediate duty-free status. For India’s agriculture and processed food sectors, elimination of tariffs on agricultural tariff lines, including fruits and vegetables, coffee, cocoa, spices, cereals and processed foods, enhances competitiveness in the New Zealand market. Engineering, manufacturing and industrial goods, transport and automotive products, electrical and electronic machinery, mechanical machinery, plastics and rubber products, and chemicals all derive tangible gains from tariff elimination or reduction, bolstering India’s diversified industrial base in exports. Boost to Pharma and Medical Devices through faster regulatory Access and this FTA designed to empower MSMEs artisans, women led enterprises and youth employment.

The Agreement between India and New Zealand is not just about goods, it includes UPI and digital payments cooperation, Faster cross-broader remittances, Support for real-time payments, more access for Indian banks and Insurance companies and up to 15 Indian bank branches allowed in New Zealand over 4 years. This strengthens India’s Fintech and Financial Services power globally.

DID YOU KNOW?

Skilled migration is considered an important factor in New Zealand’s economic growth and productivity, as migrants fill critical skill shortages, bring new capital and ideas, and contribute to the tax base.
