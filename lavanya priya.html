INDIA AND QATAR SET PATH TO DOUBLE TRADE BY 2030

India and Qatar agreed to introduce a new Free Trade Agreement (FTA) to achieve the goal of doubling their bilateral trade by 2030. According to Minister of Commerce and Industry Piyush Goyal, both India and Qatar are working to finalize the terms of reference to pave the way for a trade deal that could conclude by mid-2026. Under the Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC), Qatar is a key trading partner of India with a bilateral trade value of USD 14.15 billion.

Currently, India–Qatar trade is valued at around USD 14 billion, dominated by imports from Qatar such as crude oil and liquefied natural gas, while India’s exports remain modest at USD 1.7 billion. The new agreement aims to diversify the existing trade structure by including sectors such as electronics, automobiles, pharmaceuticals, processed foods, textiles, gems and jewellery, information technology and renewable energy.

Qatar has already invested around USD 10 billion in India during FY25 and further investments are expected. Both nations view the FTA as a strategic opportunity to enhance economic cooperation, reduce tariff and non-tariff barriers and strengthen long-term investment frameworks.

India benefits from Qatar’s strong economic linkage with Gulf countries, while Qatar gains access to India’s rapidly growing consumer and industrial market. If successfully implemented, the FTA could generate new economic growth, employment and increased bilateral trade. By combining energy security with manufacturing and technological collaboration, the India–Qatar FTA could become a cornerstone of South-West Asian economic integration and lay the foundation for long-term partnerships in the region.

Did You Know?

In 2020, Qatar became the first Gulf nation to abolish the “Exit Permit” system for most foreign workers. This reform allows employees to travel freely without employer approval and was implemented by the Government of Qatar in collaboration with the International Labour Organization (ILO).
