The New Labour Codes of India 2025, is it Favour to Labour or Not.

It was implemented by the central government on November 2025, and it replaces the 29 old labour laws and rolled out four codes for transforming workplace rights. The codes include the Code on Wages of 2019, the Industrial Relations Code of 2020, the Social Security Code of 2020, and the occupational safety, health and working conditions code of 2020.

Behind thereasons for modifying the labour laws was to simplify the registration and licensing framework by introducing the concept of an Inclusion of Gig workers, equal pay, gratuity after 1 year, Single registration, single License, and Single return, it can reduce the overall compliance burden to increasing the employment.

The second national commission on Labour has recommended that the existing Labour laws should be broadly grouped into four / five labour codes on a functional basis. Accordingly, the Ministry of labour and Employment has started the work to rationalize and simplify the relevant provisions of the labour laws in four codes.

The four labour codes were enacted after the Tripartite meeting of the government, employers, industry representatives and various trade unions from 2015 to 2019. Only the code on Wages 2019 was notified on 8th August 2019, where the remaining three codes are notified on 29th September 2020. The four new labour codes are likely to be fully operational from April 1, 2026. Now many states are opposing for many reasons, but they can’t legally reject the new labour codes.

Major problems of New Labour Codes are for Trade Unions and Instability of employment, Labours wages.

The government has announced, if the firm or company has a union with 51% membership, they will only be considered and get recognised as a negotiating union. which means ,they can bargain for their wages, salaries, and any basic facilities, work time. If a union has less than 50% of membership now, they won’t get any recognition and negotiation power.

Then, for any strike or protest which is going to be held by a union, they have to give notice before the 60 days to the management, but in old labour laws, the government mentioned the union to give notice for the strikes or protest 14 days before. The problem of the new labour law is that they have extended the days to 60 days before ,for the unions to give notice, which creates a advantage for the business people and a shutdown to the labour rights.

During the huge gap of 60 days, the industrialists may arrange a conversation between labours, threatening them by reducing the wages or replacement of jobs. Then the new labour law includes: “Mass casual leave can also be considered as paid leave during the strikes and the labours has to be punished by penalties,but it helps to prevent flash strikes and ensure lawful action for the Industrialists.”

The government also mentioned that they will fix minimum wages, and they are going to increase the basic salary for the workers, but the problem is, they have also started to increase the gratuity amount by calculating the basic salaries, so it leads to decreasing the amount of salary you will get.

If you see these things in the long term, it may be beneficial for the future, but here in the short term, if you see for the minimum wage labour, it will cause financial problems. In the long term or short term, money's value will decrease due to the factor of inflation and tax systems.

In the Old Labour Code, if a company or firm wants to close or lay off / retrenchment, it is not necessary to get approval from the government in the case of a company holding 100 or less than 100 working people in the firm or company.

But in The New Labour Code, the Government raised the approval limit from 100 to 300 workers. A firm or company can easily close or lay off their labour and their company , and the state government may also enhance the limit further. So, we can understand the situation where unemployment could easily occur and entrepreneurs and corporate people are enjoying the New Labour Codes rather than Labours.

The new labour code also replaces the punishment of imprisonment for certain first –time offenses done by the employer with monetary fines. The repeated offenses within five years, an employer can escape by paying the penalty.

Single registration can make a much easier way to start a business in the states. The states like Kerala are opposing the new labour law because of many concerns, like trade union restrictions, labour layoffs, and mainly t because single registration is more convenient for Industrialists.

On the one hand, the ease of doing business is going to be a new way of boosting the country’s economy and GDP and total employment, but at the same time it is going to affect or produce large number of negative externalities for the people and the environmental factors. Because in India, the fine or punishment for affecting the environmental things is not strictly monitored by the government.

The new labour codes do not clearly mention theworking times of labour, like 9am to 6pm or 9pm to 6am for labour. The new labour code only mentioned 8 hours / day and 48 hours / week.

It means if a person is working 8 hours in a day, he has to work 6 days in a week or if a person is working 12 hours in a day, he has to work 4 days in a week. It does not include the break hours and lunch hours in the day. The 12 hours means it is only considered as work.

The overtime will be allowed only with the worker's consent and the payment for the work.

Other than that, these New Labour Codes create some advantages and benefits for the workers in the unorganized sector and the Gig economy. These codes recognising them and give them occupational safety, health and working conditions, social security.

Then it also includes equal pay for the women's labours, and it allows them to work on night shifts, if the company provides the safety measures and basic facilities.

The central government fixed the minimum wages for the workers; the state government or the company cannot reduce or give the salary below the fixed minimum wage amount for the workers.

Then the appointment letter should be compulsory for the employee, and it has to mention the salary, pf. The migrant worker protection, contract labour regulations, are safe by the Occupational Safety, Health and Work Conditions Codes 2025.

It makes the registration process esaier for the companies ,hence can pave the way for MSME sectors to increase.
