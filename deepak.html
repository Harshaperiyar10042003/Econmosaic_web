A Prediction Error by RBI

As the adage goes, Reserve Bank of India (RBI) is the main authority in India that makes accurate predictions in order to keep the inflation rate in control. The RBI keeps the inflation rate under control (where the RBI's comfort band is 2% to 6%) because stable prices are very important for the health of the economy. However, the insight into September 2025 inflation data gives an altogether different picture. It shows at a 99-month low of 1.54% in this financial year. Except for August, all other months have shown a low inflation rate. Consistently low inflation means that supply is comfortably out shipping demand. This is the current scenario we are currently into. To counter it, the government has tried to stimulate domestic demand through an income tax cut and GST rate reduction. This is a welcoming move as it puts less burden on the people when they are purchasing a particular good and services and help the people to save more money. The people will use the extra money and consumption demand will increase, and hence private investment will rise which helps in eventually the growth of economy. It is a good news that private sector investment announcement grew strongly in the first half of this year. In midst of this, there is a policy issue the RBI needs to deal with—the inaccuracy of its forecast. In April, it predicted that inflation for the year would be 4% and in due course it revised this forecast arriving at 2.6%. Since RBI plays a key role with inflation prediction, this is a deficiency that it should address quickly.
