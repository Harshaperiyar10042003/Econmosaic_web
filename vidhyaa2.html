Green Crackers: “Are Festivals Getting Cleaner?”

The issue of air pollution automatically accompanies the festive season of Diwali, the festival of lights. The practice of bursting crackers during Diwali is followed across India and contributes significantly to air pollution and smog, often covering the sky with a thick layer the very next day after celebrations. Observing the severe health and environmental problems caused by firework crackers, scientists in India introduced green crackers as an alternative. Developed by CSIR–NEERI, green crackers are reformulated versions of conventional fireworks that exclude highly toxic metals. Scientific tests indicate that green crackers emit 30–40% less particulate matter, making them a notable step toward cleaner celebrations. However, laboratory analysis conducted after festive usage has detected residual metals that continue to contribute to pollution. Studies also reveal a sharp increase in ultrafine particles, which pose higher health risks. This raises an important question: do green crackers truly make festivals cleaner? Limiting emissions by 30% alone cannot eliminate the overall impact of pollution, nor does it make fireworks entirely eco-friendly. India remains one of the few countries permitting the use of green crackers, while many other nations have completely banned fireworks. Medical experts warn that emissions from both conventional and green crackers can aggravate respiratory and cardiovascular conditions, particularly among vulnerable groups such as children, the elderly, and those with pre-existing illnesses. During festive periods, Indian cities witness noticeable spikes in hospital visits due to breathing difficulties. Experts stress that green crackers represent a compromise rooted in tradition rather than a permanent solution. Fundamentally, the reduction in emissions during festival days does not offset pollution generated throughout the year from sources such as transport, industrial activity, and waste burning. Economically, the green crackers industry generates substantial GST revenue at a 28% tax rate during peak festive seasons and supports several downstream sectors including logistics, retail, packaging, and local chemical suppliers, thereby creating a multiplier effect across the value chain. Green crackers thus serve as an attempt to balance cultural practices with economic growth, while gradually transitioning the industry toward cleaner and more sustainable operations.
